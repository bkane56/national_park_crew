## 3. National Parks and Activities Overview

### Grand Teton National Park (10 miles from JAC)  
- Accessible year-round, including winter.  
- Activities: Scenic winter drives, cross-country skiing, snowshoeing, wildlife viewing (e.g., moose, elk), historic sites (Mormon Row), visitor center tours, and ranger-led winter programs.

### Yellowstone National Park (1-2 hours from Grand Teton NP)  
- Adjacent to Grand Teton, also open in winter with special access.  
- Activities: Guided snowmobile/snowcoach tours to geysers (Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic Spring), boardwalk hikes, wildlife watching in Lamar and Hayden Valleys, visits to visitor centers and museums.

### Bridger-Teton National Forest (Within 2 hours from Jackson)  
- Extensive forest area for winter hiking, snowshoeing, skiing.  
- Activities: Winter trails, wildlife photography, nature observation.

## 7. Recommendations and Additional Notes

- **Flight booking:** Book early for best winter rates. Allow time for layovers to accommodate winter delays.  
- **Vehicle:** Rent a 4WD vehicle equipped for snow and ice. Carry snow chains where required.  
- **Clothing:** Bring layered winter clothing, waterproof outerwear, thermal gear, gloves, hats, and appropriate footwear.  
- **Bookings:** Accommodations in Jackson Hole and West Yellowstone should be reserved well in advance, especially during holiday periods.  
- **Park passes:** Purchase passes online prior to arrival to save time.  
- **Safety:** Check weather and road conditions daily; winter park access can vary due to snow.  
- **Optional:** Consider joining ranger programs and guided tours for enhanced experience and safety in winter conditions.
